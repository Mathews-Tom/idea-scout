# SWOT & PESTLE Framework

Reference for strategic analysis synthesis in idea validation.

## SWOT Matrix Structure

| | Helpful | Harmful |
|--|---------|---------|
| **Internal** | Strengths | Weaknesses |
| **External** | Opportunities | Threats |

### Strengths (Internal, Positive)

Sources:
- Team expertise and domain knowledge
- Proprietary technology or data
- Existing customer relationships
- Cost advantages
- Speed to market

### Weaknesses (Internal, Negative)

Sources:
- Skill gaps in the team
- Limited funding or runway
- No existing distribution channel
- Technical debt or complexity
- Unproven business model

### Opportunities (External, Positive)

Sources:
- Growing market segment
- Regulatory changes enabling new products
- Technology shifts reducing barriers
- Competitor weakness or exit
- Underserved customer segment

### Threats (External, Negative)

Sources:
- Well-funded competitors
- Regulatory restrictions
- Market saturation
- Technology commoditization
- Economic downturn affecting buyer behavior

## SWOT Quality Rules

| Rule | Requirement |
|------|-------------|
| Specificity | No generic entries ("strong team" is too vague) |
| Evidence | Each entry backed by data or observation |
| Actionability | Each entry implies a strategic action |
| Balance | Minimum 3 entries per quadrant |
| No overlap | Each entry in exactly one quadrant |

## PESTLE Analysis

| Factor | What to Assess | Example Questions |
|--------|---------------|-------------------|
| Political | Government policy, trade, stability | Tariffs? Subsidies? Political risk? |
| Economic | Growth, inflation, exchange rates | Recession impact? Pricing pressure? |
| Social | Demographics, culture, trends | Changing consumer behavior? |
| Technological | Innovation, automation, R&D | Enabling technology maturing? Disruption risk? |
| Legal | Regulation, compliance, IP | Licensing requirements? Data privacy laws? |
| Environmental | Sustainability, climate, resources | ESG expectations? Carbon regulations? |

## PESTLE Scoring

| Level | Definition |
|-------|-----------|
| Favorable | Factor actively supports the business |
| Neutral | Factor has no significant impact |
| Unfavorable | Factor creates headwinds or risk |
| Blocking | Factor could prevent the business from operating |

## Cross-Referencing SWOT and PESTLE

Map PESTLE findings into SWOT:

| PESTLE Factor | Maps To |
|---------------|---------|
| Favorable external factor | Opportunity |
| Unfavorable external factor | Threat |
| Factor team can leverage | Strength (if capable) or Weakness (if not) |
| Blocking factor | Threat (may be NO-GO signal) |

## Porter's Five Forces (Competitive Supplement)

| Force | High = Bad For Entrant | Assessment |
|-------|----------------------|------------|
| Threat of new entrants | Low barriers, easy to copy | HIGH / MEDIUM / LOW |
| Bargaining power of suppliers | Few suppliers, high switching cost | HIGH / MEDIUM / LOW |
| Bargaining power of buyers | Many alternatives, low switching cost | HIGH / MEDIUM / LOW |
| Threat of substitutes | Alternative solutions available | HIGH / MEDIUM / LOW |
| Industry rivalry | Many competitors, slow growth | HIGH / MEDIUM / LOW |

## Strategic Implications Template

For each SWOT quadrant, derive strategic actions:

| Combination | Strategy |
|-------------|----------|
| Strength + Opportunity | Leverage (invest and accelerate) |
| Strength + Threat | Defend (use strengths to mitigate threats) |
| Weakness + Opportunity | Improve (address weakness to capture opportunity) |
| Weakness + Threat | Avoid or pivot (highest risk quadrant) |
